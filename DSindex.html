Strata Title Separation Checklist

A step-by-step guide to separating your property from a strata title in South Australia.

Step What Why How Completed
1 Review strata plan and bylaws Understand shared responsibilities and restrictions Obtain documents from Lands Titles Office or strata manager
2 Engage a licensed surveyor Assess subdivision feasibility and prepare a new plan Contact a surveyor to evaluate the property and draft a subdivision plan
3 Consult a town planner Understand council requirements for subdivision Discuss zoning, planning policies, and approval processes with a town planner
4 Engage a strata/conveyancing lawyer Navigate legal processes and lodge applications Work with a lawyer to draft documents, negotiate with other owners, and submit applications
5 Submit subdivision plan to council Obtain planning approval for the subdivision Lodge the subdivision plan with the local council for review and approval
6 Negotiate with other strata owners Obtain consent to amend the strata plan Discuss the proposal with other owners and seek unanimous or special resolution approval
7 Lodge documents with Lands Titles Office Finalize the new title for your property Submit the approved subdivision plan and legal documents to the Lands Titles Office
8 Update shared services and easements Ensure proper management of sewage, stormwater, and other shared services Work with an engineer to reconfigure services and formalize easements if necessary
